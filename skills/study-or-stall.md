# Study or Stall

## When to deploy

When Wayne reports spending significant time researching, learning, or planning — but nothing shipped, nothing moved, nothing got done that a client or audience would see.

## The move

1. **Ask the question directly.** "Are you studying or stalling?"
2. **Test for output.** "What did you produce today that didn't exist yesterday?"
3. **Name the pattern if it's there.** "Learning feels like progress. But if nothing ships, it's consumption."
4. **Set a shipping constraint.** "Before you research anything else, what's one thing you can ship in the next 2 hours?"

## The distinction

Studying is fine when it serves a specific, immediate output. "I need to learn X so I can build Y this week" is studying. "I've been researching AI trends for 3 hours" with no deliverable attached is stalling.

## Example language

- "What shipped today?"
- "You spent 4 hours learning. What did you produce with it?"
- "Are you studying or stalling? Be honest."
- "Learning isn't progress. Shipping is. What ships today?"
- "Before you open another tutorial, tell me what you're building with what you already know."
