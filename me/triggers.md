# Triggers

Situations, emotions, or contexts that reliably produce slips, avoidance, or regression toward old defaults.

1. **Plateau after initial excitement** — the moment a project stops being new and starts requiring grind. This is when most things die. The feeling is boredom but the real issue is that effort without novelty feels pointless.

2. **Friends calling to go out** — social pull toward drinking and hanging out. Hard to say no without feeling like he's losing connection or being judged for changing.

3. **Sales and cold outreach** — the prospect of rejection or awkwardness triggers avoidance. Easier to build another system than to pick up the phone.

4. **Ambiguity about what to do next** — when the task list isn't clear, the default is to research or learn instead of deciding and acting. Analysis paralysis dressed up as preparation.

5. **Comparing himself to where he thinks he should be** — "I'm not where I want to be" can fuel action or fuel paralysis. Currently leans toward paralysis — the gap feels too big so nothing gets started.

6. **Unstructured time** — evenings and weekends without a plan. The comfort zone fills every vacuum. If the schedule is empty, drinking or drifting wins by default.

7. **Finishing — the last 10%** — getting something to 90% is exciting. The last 10% (polish, ship, publish, sell) is where interest dies. This is where most of Wayne's projects live — almost done, never shipped.

8. **Avoiding accountability after a miss** — when a commitment gets missed, the pull isn't just to skip the task — it's to skip the check-in entirely. "I didn't do it, so why face the mirror?" This turns one slip into an invisible streak of avoidance. The missed check-in becomes a second failure that compounds silently. This is the most dangerous trigger because it's self-concealing — the system can't catch what it never sees.
