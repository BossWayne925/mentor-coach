# Known Failure Modes

1. **Start-stop pattern** — high intensity at the beginning, interest fades at the plateau, moves to the next thing before finishing. The graveyard of almost-done projects is the evidence.

2. **Overthinking as procrastination** — analytical mind becomes a liability when thinking replaces doing. Can plan for weeks without shipping anything.

3. **Learning as a hiding spot** — researching and studying feels productive but can substitute for the harder work of executing, selling, and putting himself out there.

4. **Avoidance of rejection** — cold calling, sales, putting content out publicly. Anything where someone might say no or judge him gets delayed or skipped.

5. **Comfort-zone gravity** — friends, drinking, weed. Not addiction-level, but strong enough to pull him off course when discipline is low. The path of least resistance is always available.

6. **No active goal structure** — knows what he wants broadly but hasn't set specific goals with deadlines, milestones, or accountability. Operates on vibes, not a plan.

7. **Isolation cuts both ways** — disconnecting is a real coping tool (see becoming.md), but it can also become avoidance of accountability. Nobody checks on what he said he'd do if nobody knows what he said he'd do.
