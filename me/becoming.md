# Who I'm Becoming

## The portrait

A disciplined builder who finishes what he starts. Runs EastBay Creative Solutions as a real business — not a side hustle, not a hobby with an LLC. Revenue comes from AI operating systems and workstations for local SMBs, not one-off website builds. Has a content presence on YouTube that attracts clients and builds authority. Ships consistently — videos, systems, client work — because he has a routine that protects his focus.

A present father who cooks dinner for his son every evening and models what it looks like to build something from nothing with discipline instead of chaos.

A man who chose himself over the comfort zone. Doesn't drink. Doesn't drift. Doesn't hang with people who aren't building. Not out of arrogance — out of protection. He knows what pulls him back.

## What his days look like

- Morning: sun exposure, water with sea salt and lemon/lime. No food until noon (intermittent fasting).
- Walk, nature, meditation before work starts.
- Work block starts 8-9 AM. Task list is set the night before. The list gets completed, not browsed.
- Afternoon: client work, content creation, or system building — whichever the plan says, not whichever feels interesting.
- Evening: cook dinner with his son. Present, not distracted.
- Close of day: reflection. Three questions — What could I have done better? What needs to happen tomorrow? Am I actually working toward my goals or just feeling busy?
- Says no to: going out drinking, sugar and processed food, anyone who isn't on the same level or at least pointed in the same direction.

## How he handles hard moments

When interest fades or the pull to drift hits, he disconnects. Phone off. Unavailable. Not antisocial — strategic isolation. He knows the difference between loneliness and solitude.

Replacement behaviors: works out, walks, hikes, researches something that feeds the mission. He channels restless energy into movement and learning instead of numbing.

He doesn't fight the urge to quit by white-knuckling. He changes his environment — removes the triggers, fills the space with something that serves the becoming.
