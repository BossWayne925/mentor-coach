# If-Then Plans

Pre-loaded responses for known triggers (from `me/triggers.md`). The point is to decide *before* the moment hits, so willpower isn't the only defense.

## Trigger → Pre-loaded response

**IF** I hit the plateau on a project and it stops feeling exciting,
**THEN** I open `goals/active-arc.md`, re-read what "done" looks like, and do 25 minutes of focused work on the hardest remaining task. Excitement isn't required. Identity is.

**IF** friends call to go out drinking,
**THEN** I say "not tonight, I'm locked in" — no explanation, no negotiation. If I need social time, I suggest a walk or a meal instead.

**IF** I need to do cold outreach or sales and I feel the avoidance,
**THEN** I set a timer for 15 minutes and do nothing but outreach. I don't have to like it. I have to do it. 15 minutes, then I can stop.

**IF** I don't know what to do next and the task list is empty,
**THEN** I open `goals/weekly-commitments.md` and pick the commitment I'm furthest behind on. If nothing's behind, I open `goals/90-day-picture.md` and ask: "What moves the needle most right now?"

**IF** I catch myself comparing where I am to where I think I should be,
**THEN** I read `me/becoming.md` once, then ask: "What's one thing I can do in the next hour that the man I'm becoming would do?" Then I do it.

**IF** I have unstructured time (evening/weekend) with no plan,
**THEN** I check `goals/weekly-commitments.md`. If everything's on track, I earn the free time. If not, I work first.

**IF** a project hits 90% and I feel the pull to move on,
**THEN** I name it out loud: "This is the last 10%. This is where I usually stop." Then I list the specific tasks to finish and do one of them right now. Finishing IS the identity test.
